Angels Of War is a website that commemorates Australian Army Nurses and promotes an exhibition of staff/student work. In partnership with FedSquare, the website tells stories of the contributions and sacrifices made by the nurses through articles, a timeline of events and a gallery of images.

The magazine and newspaper-inspired layout, dusty colours and old photographs nod to the historical context. While white space balances out the text-heavy content and adds a fresh and clean look in line with the medical field. It encourages reflection and an appreciation of Australian Army Nurses.

Images top to bottom, left to right

1 The desktop website home screen promoting the exhibit. The combination of fonts and monotonous header images set a tone of elegance. The menu bar has a white background to ensure visibility at all times.

2 An acknowledgement of country and viewer discretion on the desktop and mobile website. The popup only appears when a new browser is opened.

3 The directory page for stories. Apart from the feature article, each story focuses on a nurse. The blog-like format makes it the only desktop page that is vertically symmetrical.

4 Mobile view for "The Vyner Brooke Tragedy" article. All columns default to a single column and images in the body are full width. The header background colour is repeated at the end of the story to signal a closing. The top navigation bar also stacks into a hamburger menu.

5 Each story uses a colour from the overall palette. The timeline page also follows the same rule where each year, set in full height, is separated by a different colour.

6 An example of layout on the desktop website. Columns are flexible to break up the text and stand-out paragraphs are highlighted with an accent font. No two pages are laid out the same.
