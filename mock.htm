Mockingbird is a Google Chrome extension for desktop Twitter. It changes the headings and names of key functions into their literal intentions. Inspired by Jenny Odell's ideas on the attention economy, it exposes how social media pushes users to be emotionally invested and waste time. Each trigger and label was chosen with user interaction in mind.

The extension is no longer available on the Chrome store as of 2021.

1 The changed headings and text.

2 The delay in change upon loading Twitter. It serves as an alarm to awaken obsessive Twitter users who are oblivious to the amount of energy they pour into social media. Ironically, it draws attention to the altered labels and invokes a realisation of the platform's motives, where they grab our attention to force users to stay.

3 A scroll interaction that triggers the 'Wormhole' on the home page. It plays on the idea that Twitter sucks you into endless scrolling. The same thing occurs where the headings on the notifications, bookmarks and lists pages are changed upon scroll.

4 A typing interaction that causes the Tweet button to change to 'Surrender'.

5 A click interaction where a chat on the messages page needs to be clicked on to transform it into the 'Restroom'. These interactions ask users to think twice before engaging with the platform, in hopes of deterring them from frequent usage and lurking.
