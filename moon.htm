The Moon Is Made Of Cheese is a title sequence for a hypothetical sci-fi comedy sitcom. It features 2D animations influenced by the physics of surrealism. Through lively and bouncy movements, there is a sense of playfulness and curiosity.

Using visual juxtapositions, the title sequence plays with reflections, shadows and mirrors to express the character's wavering worlds.

The soundtrack, remixed from "The Mountain" by Podington Bear↗, supports the idea of having two sides of the same coin. The narrative was constructed around the four bars of the soundtrack. Each of these four sections in the animation represents a piece of the character's journey.

Synopsis

An anxious soul, who unconsciously alternates between two worlds when she wakes up after sleeping, wishes to only stay in one. However, when her dreams come true and she is stuck there, she begins to question its reality. Now, she must find where she belongs.

Media top to bottom, left to right

2-4 Scene one: identity and belonging. The title sequence introduces the character's abilities through the visual motif of a moon where the world she knows of splits into two. One half of the moon falls through, while the other half melts.

5-8 Scene two: freedom and exploration. Orange and yellow illuminate the openness and flexibility of the universe. Soft lines and natural shapes/objects like the sun, trees and water suggest the character's purity and innocence.

9-12 Scene three: doubt and confinement. Blue and green scenes portray the character's struggles. Sharp edges and blocks of concrete represent the internal conflict she experiences.

13 Scene four: a still frame of the title. The Moon Is Made Of Cheese concludes with the notion of balance and discovery by accepting what you have (the moon) and looking at it with hope (cheese) despite the challenges of reality.
