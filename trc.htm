The Reading Club↗ is an interactive website that explores the connection between the brain and the digital. Inspired by the aesthetics of Net Art, it addresses and critiques the way the rapid processes of the Internet have shifted our reading habits.

The website was optimised for Google Chrome (1920x1080) and is no longer updated as of 2021.



Completed
November 2019

Purpose
Student Project

Type
Desktop Website Design & Development

Languages

Images top to bottom, left to right

1 The website main screen. It introduces a questionnaire about an imaginary book club that users must complete in order to successfully join the online community.

2 Website interaction using the mouse and click/drag movement. The boxes take the form of 3D rectangular prisms to mimic advertisements. The quiz is based on riddles and nonsense questions that were paraphrased to fit the context. They are intentionally difficult to ensure that only incorrect answers are given.

3 Progress through the quiz. When the user selects the incorrect answer, more prisms appear which simulates the inconvenience of pop up ads. The amount of text also multiples. These distractions challenge the reading comprehension and concentration of the user. This reflects our expectations and routine practices in reading that have sped up to match the fast-paced mechanisms of the web and its disturbing format.

4-5 Browser pop up boxes implemented to guide users. Those who select the correct answers receive an error message, and those who reach the end of the quiz receive a congratulatory message.
